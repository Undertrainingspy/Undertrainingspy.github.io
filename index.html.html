What are Bomb Cyclones

For this topic of the assignment, I chose an article from Reuters by Will Dunham. This article delves into the concept of bomb cyclones, a powerful weather phenomenon with the potential to cause significant damage. According to the article, bomb cyclones are mid-latitude cyclones that rapidly intensify, creating hurricane-force winds, heavy precipitation, and severe weather conditions. These storms are particularly common during the cold season and are fueled by a combination of atmospheric and oceanic conditions, such as a strong jet stream and warm ocean surfaces.
